{% trans 'The different types of Summer Camps' %}

{% blocktrans %}

There are two types of Girls Can Code! summer camps:

  • Long ones, they last for a week. They were first created in 2014.
  • Short ones, also called "Girls Can Code! Week-end", they started in 2019.
{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Content and schedule of the week-end Summer Camps' %}

{% blocktrans %} They are shorter thus give less time to discover the variety of computer science but can be organized more often and in various locations. They can focus on special topics but as a default they are an introduction to computer science via practical sessions. The first day focuses on the discovery of Python and the next on the creation of a small project on a microcontroller. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}Here is an example schedule of a GCC! week-end, but the time of arrival and departure may vary, you will be sent a precise one when accepted.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Content and schedule of the week-long Summer Camps' %}

{% blocktrans %}The summer camps lasts for one week and mainly consist of practical exercises. The week starts with an initiation to the Python programming language, and then some introductive tutorials about several subdomains of informatics like network or web design. Finally, the week ends with a personal project, that can be chosen among several main topics: video game, robotics or microcontroller.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}Here is a typical schedule of a GCC! week. Notice that the attendees can arrive between 9 am and 9:30 am and can leave at between 18 pm and 19 am.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}Prologin can attribute grants for some of the participants. Specifically, Prologin can pay back the costs of the transport and housing for participants, thus they need to keep their receipts.{% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %}first and last name of the participant{% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %}whether you need this grant for the travel, the housing or both {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %}a proof of address{% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %}a your last tax notice{% endblocktrans %}
